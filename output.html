SECTION 7 — Experimental Performance Analysis
Timing unit: microseconds (μs)
Source node: 0 for all traversal/SSSP algorithms

Graph Sizes:
  Small Sparse: 10 nodes, 12 edges
  Small Dense:  10 nodes, 45 edges
  Large Sparse: 50 nodes, 65 edges
  Large Dense:  50 nodes, 1225 edges

----------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Algorithm     | Small Sparse    | Small Dense     | Large Sparse    | Large Dense
----------------------------------------------------------------------------------
BFS           | 0.561μs        | 0.231μs        | 0.260μs        | 1.644μs
DFS           | 0.250μs        | 0.180μs        | 0.361μs        | 1.623μs
Dijkstra      | 0.461μs        | 0.471μs        | 1.112μs        | 3.317μs
Bellman-Ford  | 0.170μs        | 0.140μs        | 0.611μs        | 1.644μs
Kruskal       | 0.541μs        | 1.172μs        | 2.194μs        | 50.466μs
----------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Key Observations:
  - BFS/DFS are fastest (simple traversal, O(V+E))
  - Dijkstra is efficient for sparse graphs (O((V+E) log V))
  - Bellman-Ford is slowest on dense graphs (O(V*E))
  - Kruskal scales with edges (O(E log E))
